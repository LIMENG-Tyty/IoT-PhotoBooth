Behind the Magic

How It Works

A peek under the hood of our IoT photo booth system.

1

ESP32-CAM Streams Video

The ESP32-CAM broadcasts a live MJPEG stream over Wi-Fi at http://esp32cam-a1b2.local:81/stream. The feed is proxied through Flask and displayed here in real time.

2

MediaPipe Detects Gestures

Each frame is analysed by Google's MediaPipe Hands library. When all five fingers are extended for more than one second — the High-Five is confirmed and the sequence begins.

3

Ring Light & Countdown

The ESP32 ring light illuminates for the countdown, then fires a bright flash burst at capture time — providing perfect fill light from the WS2812B ring.

4

Frame Overlay Applied

Python (OpenCV) composites your chosen frame style and colour directly onto the JPEG before saving — so the final image has the border baked in.

5

Photo Delivered to Telegram

The framed image is immediately pushed to a Telegram bot which forwards it to your chat. No app installs needed — scan a QR code and receive your memory instantly.

Frequently Asked
Do I need a Telegram account?
Yes — simply open the bot link or scan the QR code in under 30 seconds and you're ready to receive photos.
What if the gesture isn't detected?
Make sure all five fingers are clearly spread, you're well-lit, and within 0.5–1.5 m of the camera. Lighting is the biggest factor for the wide-angle ESP32-CAM lens.
Can I take multiple shots?
Absolutely — after each photo the system resets automatically. Lower your hand for two seconds then raise it again to start a new sequence.
Is the frame style applied in the actual photo?
Yes — the frame overlay is composited on the server using Python / OpenCV before the image is sent to Telegram, so your chosen style is baked right into the final photo.

Our Story

About Us

A team of IoT enthusiasts blending hardware craft with vintage aesthetics.

The Project

SnapBooth was born as our IoT final project — a smart photo booth that merges embedded systems with computer vision. We wanted something that feels magical to use: no buttons, no screens to touch — just you, a hand in the air, and a perfect memory delivered straight to your phone.

The vintage aesthetic was a deliberate choice. The ESP32-CAM produces warm, slightly grainy imagery that feels closer to analogue photography than modern digital. We leaned into that character rather than fighting it.
